美股半导体 AI 方向深度 Survey：继续上涨的信号与反方论证

日期：2026-05-14

说明：本文是产业与市场研究，不构成投资建议。由于部分 PDF/IR 页面在当前环境中只能抓到二进制或 JS 页面，本文优先采用可直接访问的公司公告、产业研究页面、Tavily/Web 检索摘要与并行研究代理交叉验证；对单一来源或争议性估算会明确标注。

核心结论

美股半导体的 AI 交易已经从“买 GPU”变成“买 AI 基础设施瓶颈”。如果未来继续上涨，最强的信号大概率不只来自 Nvidia 单家公司，而会来自一组可验证的链条：hyperscaler 继续上调 capex，GPU/ASIC 订单仍被先进制程和封装约束，HBM 与 DRAM/NAND 价格维持强势，网络互连、电力/散热、测试/良率控制成为新的短板。

但反方也变得更具体：它不是简单说“AI 是泡沫”，而是问四个问题：云厂商是否赚得回这轮资本开支？GPU 的真实经济寿命是否短于账面折旧？HBM/封装扩产在 2027 年后是否会反噬价格？市场是否已经把高增长、高毛利、高份额和低利率环境一次性都定价进去了？

因此，当前更像一个“基本面仍强，但容错率下降”的阶段。最值得跟踪的不是单日股价，而是订单、capex、价格、良率、毛利率和云端 AI 收入披露之间是否继续互相印证。

一、为什么 AI 半导体仍有继续上涨的产业信号

1. AI capex 仍是整条链的源头变量

IDC 在 2026 年 4 月的半导体展望中预计，全球半导体收入将在 2026 年达到 1.29 万亿美元，同比增 52.8%，并称 AI infrastructure 已经成为结构性主导需求；其摘要还提到 hyperscale capex 在 2025 年 Q3 首次超过 1000 亿美元，2026 年 i4 capex 预计同比增长约 70%，接近 6000 亿美元。来源：IDC。

MUFG 的 AI financing 周报也给出类似方向，称 2026 年 Big Five hyperscaler capex 预计超过 6000 亿美元，其中约 75%、即 4500 亿美元直接与 AI infrastructure 有关。来源：MUFG PDF。

这条信号的意义在于：半导体收入是云厂商资本开支的上游镜像。只要 Amazon、Microsoft、Google、Meta、Oracle 等继续扩 AI 数据中心，GPU、custom ASIC、HBM、先进封装、交换机、光互连、电力设备和测试设备就都还有需求支撑。反过来，如果这些公司的 capex guidance 出现连续下修，AI 半导体的估值基础会迅速动摇。

2. Nvidia 仍在用收入和毛利证明需求没有明显降温

Nvidia 2026 财年 Q4 公告显示，季度收入 681 亿美元，同比增 73%；数据中心收入 623 亿美元，同比增 75%；全年收入 2159 亿美元，同比增 65%。公司同时披露 Q4 GAAP gross margin 为 75.0%。来源：Nvidia SEC 8-K / Q4 FY2026 release。

Jensen Huang 在同一公告中称：

“Computing demand is growing exponentially — the agentic AI inflection point has arrived.”

这类管理层表述当然带有销售属性，但收入、数据中心增速和高毛利共同说明：至少到 2026 年初，市场还没有看到典型半导体下行周期中“价格先松、毛利先掉、订单能见度下降”的组合。

继续上涨的正面信号是：Nvidia 的网络收入、系统级收入和下一代 Rubin 平台可见度继续增强，而不是只靠单一 GPU ASP。反方则要盯毛利率是否从 70%+ 区间明显回落，以及供应链扩张后客户是否开始压价。

3. AI 不是只有 Nvidia：custom ASIC 与推理经济性可能扩大受益面

AMD 2026 Q1 公告称，公司一季度表现由 AI infrastructure 需求加速驱动，Data Center 已成为收入和盈利增长的主要驱动；公司给出 2026 Q2 收入中值 112 亿美元，同比增约 46%，non-GAAP gross margin 约 56%。Lisa Su 还提到 inferencing 和 agentic AI 正推动 CPU 与 accelerator 需求，MI450 Series 和 Helios 客户预测超过初始预期。来源：AMD Q1 2026 results。

这意味着 AI 半导体的第二阶段可能从“训练 GPU 短缺”转向“训练 + 推理 + agentic workload + 定制芯片”。Broadcom、Marvell 一类 custom silicon / networking 供应商的逻辑也在这里：当推理成本成为核心变量，云厂商会更愿意用 ASIC 优化特定 workload。

这对行业是双刃剑：对 Broadcom、Marvell、TSMC、EDA/IP 是扩散利好；对 Nvidia 则是长期份额和毛利率的潜在压力。最好的多头信号是 ASIC 放量并没有压垮 GPU，而是共同扩大 AI compute TAM。

4. HBM 和内存可能是 2026 年最硬的瓶颈之一

IDC 把 memory 描述为本轮变化的核心，预计 2026 年 DRAM 收入达到 4186 亿美元，受 HBM 与 AI infrastructure 拉动显著增长，并称 HBM 已成为 AI accelerator supply chain 的主要约束之一。来源：IDC。

Deloitte 的 2026 semiconductor outlook 也强调，AI 训练和推理对 HBM3、HBM4、DDR7 的需求造成其他消费内存短缺，部分 DDR4/DDR5 价格在 2025 年 9 月至 11 月间上涨约 4 倍，并提示 2026 年上半年仍可能继续涨价。来源：Deloitte 2026 Semiconductor Industry Outlook。

Micron 的投资者材料也给出更直接的公司层面信号：AI demand 推动 DRAM 和 NAND data center bit TAM 在 2026 年首次超过行业 TAM 的 50%；公司称 AI 与传统 server 需求都受 DRAM/NAND 供应不足约束，并预计 2026 财年 capex 超过 250 亿美元。来源：Micron investor material。

HBM 的特殊之处在于，它不是普通内存周期的简单涨价版。它需要高良率堆叠、先进封装、长期客户 qualification，并且会挤占标准 DRAM wafer capacity。多头如果成立，Micron 这类公司可能被重新定价为“AI 关键瓶颈供应商”，而不是传统强周期内存股。

5. 先进封装、测试、良率控制从后端配角变成主线

Tavily 初扫与多条外部检索都指向同一件事：AI 芯片的限制不只在晶圆前端，也在 CoWoS、先进封装、HBM stacking、测试和良率控制。KLA 相关 earnings 摘要显示，公司 advanced wafer-level packaging 市场份额提升，并预计 advanced packaging process-control revenue 从 2025 年约 6.35 亿美元增至 2026 年约 10 亿美元。来源：Yahoo Finance / KLA Q3 2026 earnings summary。

TSMC 的 2026 资本开支计划也提供间接验证。Bloomberg 报道 TSMC 计划 2026 年 capex 达 520-560 亿美元，并预计 2026 年收入增长接近 30%，这被市场解读为 AI 需求持续强劲。来源：Bloomberg 摘要页。

这条链上值得关注的公司包括 TSM、ASML、AMAT、LRCX、KLAC、Teradyne，以及先进封装 OSAT/材料/测试相关公司。继续上涨的信号不是一句“AI 很热”，而是先进封装产能仍被预订、设备订单 backlog 延长、良率工具收入加速。

6. 网络、光互连、电力和散热成为 AI 数据中心的新约束

Nvidia Q4 FY2026 公告把数据中心收入拆分为 compute 与 networking，TechCrunch 对其财报的报道提到季度数据中心收入中有约 110 亿美元来自 NVLink 等 networking 产品。来源：TechCrunch。

这说明 AI cluster 不只是“买更多 GPU”。大规模推理和训练需要低延迟网络、交换机、光模块、功率管理、液冷、机房电力和数据中心建设。若未来市场从 chip picker 转向 infrastructure basket，ANET、MRVL、AVGO、MPWR、VRT 等可能继续受益。

要验证这条链，需看两个信号：第一，networking revenue 是否快于传统企业网络周期；第二，电力/散热公司 backlog 是否来自 AI data center，而不是普通工业周期。

二、未来继续涨的观察清单

如果要判断美股半导体 AI 方向是否还有上行空间，我会把信号按强弱分为三层。

第一层是需求硬信号：hyperscaler capex guidance 上修或不下修；Nvidia/AMD/Broadcom/Marvell 订单能见度延长；Micron/SK hynix/Samsung 的 HBM 供给被提前锁定；TSMC advanced node 与 CoWoS 产能仍供不应求。

第二层是价格与利润信号：Nvidia 数据中心毛利率维持高位；Micron HBM 与 DRAM/NAND pricing 继续改善；设备公司订单和服务收入同步增强；网络和电力设备公司 backlog 含金量提升。

第三层是扩散信号：AI 收入不再只集中于 Nvidia，而是扩散到 ASIC、EDA、封装、测试、内存、网络、功率和散热。扩散越健康，板块越不像单一龙头交易；扩散如果只是低质量补涨，风险反而上升。

三、反方论证：为什么这轮上涨可能会出问题

1. 估值容错率已经下降

AI 半导体核心公司的股价已经不只是反映“今年增长好”，而是反映未来多年高增长、高份额和高毛利率维持。熊方最强的点在于：即便基本面没有崩，只要增长从极高速度回落到“仍然很好但低于预期”，估值倍数也可能压缩。

这类风险在 Nvidia 最典型，也扩散到 Broadcom、AMD、Micron、TSMC、ASML 等。多头可以说盈利增长会消化估值；反方则会问，当前价格是否已经把 2027-2028 的乐观情景提前买完。

2. 云厂商 ROI 仍不透明

J.P. Morgan Asset Management 在 2026 outlook 中把 hyperscaler AI 投资的“metaverse moment”列为重要风险：四大 hyperscaler 自 2022 年 Q4 以来在 capex 与 R&D 上投入约 1.3 万亿美元，但 AI 投资是否能带来相称利润仍需要验证；报告还提到 free cash flow margin 下滑和现金余额下降使 2026 年更需要清晰盈利路径。来源：J.P. Morgan Asset Management PDF。

这是真正的关键反方：半导体厂商今天确认收入，云厂商未来承担折旧、电力和利用率风险。如果 AI 应用收入不能覆盖资本成本，芯片订单会从“越多越好”切换到“先消化库存和产能”。

3. 折旧与 GPU 经济寿命可能成为利润压力

部分怀疑派认为 AI GPU 技术迭代太快，真实经济寿命可能短于云厂商账面折旧年限。若 5-6 年折旧假设过于乐观，云厂商的当前利润可能低估了未来设备更新、减值和闲置风险。

多头的反证是：旧 GPU 在推理、内部 workload、低端模型和云租赁市场仍有高利用率，且 token demand 足够大，能够让不同代际设备分层使用。如果未来二手 GPU 价格、云 GPU 租赁价格和利用率都保持坚挺，折旧熊方会被削弱。

4. HBM 与内存仍逃不开供给周期

HBM 今天稀缺，不代表永远稀缺。Micron、SK hynix、Samsung 都在扩大 HBM 和 cleanroom 投资。Deloitte 也提醒，DRAM/NAND capex 若因价格上涨而进一步增加，可能再次制造过剩。来源：Deloitte。

Micron 的低 forward P/E 看似便宜，但内存股在周期高点常常看起来最便宜，因为市场预期峰值盈利不可持续。反方要看的不是 2026 年 EPS 有多高，而是 2027 年以后 HBM4 竞争、Samsung qualification、普通 DRAM/NAND 供给恢复后，价格是否还能维持。

5. Custom ASIC 是扩散机会，也是 Nvidia 的长期替代风险

ASIC 的兴起可以扩大 AI 半导体受益面，但也可能改变利润分配。Google TPU、Amazon Trainium、自研 inference ASIC、Broadcom 定制芯片、Marvell 定制互连方案，都会把一部分价值从通用 GPU 转向客户和定制硅供应链。

短期内，这未必冲击 Nvidia，因为 GPU 生态、CUDA、NVLink、系统集成和 time-to-market 仍强。但长期如果推理 workload 标准化、模型架构稳定、客户更重视每 token 成本，ASIC 对 GPU 毛利率和份额的压力会变大。

6. 中国限制、台湾风险和供应链集中仍是不可忽视的尾部风险

Nvidia 在 Q4 FY2026 公告中提到，其中国 H200 产品虽有少量获批，但尚未产生收入，公司也不确定相关产品是否能被允许进口到中国。来源：Nvidia SEC 8-K / Q4 FY2026 release。

与此同时，TSMC 在领先制程和先进封装上的集中地位让整个 AI 半导体链暴露于台湾地缘风险。美国出口管制可能限制中国收入，但也可能促使中国自研替代加速。这个风险平时难以定价，一旦爆发会迅速重估整条链。

四、未来板块方向地图：机会不只在 GPU，而在瓶颈迁移

更系统地看，AI 半导体未来的主线不是一个方向，而是“瓶颈迁移”：先是 GPU 不够，随后 HBM 不够、CoWoS/先进封装不够、N3/N2 先进制程不够、网络带宽不够、电力和散热不够。资金往往会追逐当前最卡脖子的环节，而不是固定停留在某一类公司。

Omdia 的 2026 semiconductor trends 把数据中心服务器列为半导体收入的主导驱动，明确提到 GPU、logic ASSP/ASIC、HBM、power management IC 都受 AI 数据中心拉动；同时指出 2026 年主题包括更大规模训练、CSP 继续扩容、老服务器升级、电力/冷却改善和 edge AI 增长。来源：Omdia 摘要。

TrendForce 的 AI server outlook 也把 2026 年重点放在 hyperscaler capex、NVIDIA rack-scale 平台、US CSP 自研 ASIC、HBM/CoWoS 紧张、液冷主流化和中美限制带来的本土化。来源：TrendForce。

方向 1：训练 GPU / 通用 AI 加速平台

  • 代表公司：NVDA、AMD。
  • 为什么重要： frontier model 训练、后训练、强化学习、长上下文和 agentic AI 仍需要高端 GPU 集群。Nvidia 当前不只是 GPU，而是 GPU + NVLink/NVSwitch + networking + rack-scale system + CUDA 的平台。
  • 继续上涨的条件：Blackwell/Rubin 出货继续超预期；推理并未削弱 GPU 需求，反而因 token consumption 增长扩大总算力；数据中心毛利维持高位；networking 与系统收入占比提升。
  • 重点观察：Nvidia data center revenue/gross margin；Blackwell/Rubin 交期；云 GPU 租赁价格和利用率；AMD MI 系列客户数量、收入和毛利；CUDA/ROCm 软件生态进展。
  • 主要风险：估值最充分；毛利率对供给释放和客户压价敏感；ASIC 在可预测推理 workload 中替代部分 GPU；出口管制影响中国收入。

方向 2：推理加速器与 custom ASIC

  • 代表公司：AVGO、MRVL、AMD，间接受益 TSM；云厂商自研包括 Google TPU、Amazon Trainium/Inferentia、Microsoft Maia、Meta MTIA。
  • 为什么重要：AI 从训练进入大规模推理后，关键指标从“绝对性能”转向“每 token 成本、每瓦性能、可部署规模”。Creative Strategies 把 2026 框架拆成 capex 可持续性、compute 到 energy 的约束迁移、推理经济性、自研硅扩张、memory/packaging 物理瓶颈。来源：Creative Strategies。
  • 继续上涨的条件：Broadcom/Marvell custom silicon backlog 上修；云厂商 TPU/Trainium/Maia/MTIA 从内部实验进入大规模部署；ASIC 与 GPU 是增量扩容而非零和替代。
  • 重点观察：AVGO/MRVL AI revenue 或 custom silicon backlog；云厂商自研芯片代际发布与部署规模；ASIC 使用的 HBM/CoWoS allocation；推理 API 成本下降后调用量是否更快增长。
  • 主要风险：项目制收入集中在少数 hyperscaler；自研芯片可能把利润留给云厂商；如果 ASIC 替代 GPU 而不是扩大市场，会压缩 NVDA 估值。

方向 3：HBM、DRAM、NAND 与 AI 存储层

  • 代表公司：MU；海外对应 SK hynix、Samsung。
  • 为什么重要：HBM 是训练和高端推理的硬瓶颈，长上下文、agentic workflow、checkpoint、RAG 和多模态数据还会拉动 DRAM/NAND/enterprise SSD。IDC 预计 2026 年 DRAM 收入因 HBM 与 DDR 需求大幅增长，并称 HBM 容量已大多预订到 2026、部分延伸到 2027。来源：IDC。
  • 继续上涨的条件：HBM4/HBM4E qualification 顺利；长期供货协议、预付款、价格地板增多；HBM 挤占标准 DRAM wafer capacity，带动普通 DRAM/NAND 也紧。
  • 重点观察：Micron HBM revenue/share、HBM4 量产与 Nvidia/AMD qualification；HBM ASP 与合约期限；DRAM/NAND 现货与合约价；enterprise SSD 价格和供给；capex 是否从“纪律扩产”变成“抢产能”。
  • 主要风险：内存仍是强周期行业；Samsung 放量或客户库存累积可能导致价格回落；高 capex 在 2027 后制造过剩。

方向 4：先进封装、CoWoS、chiplets 与 2.5D/3D integration

  • 代表公司：TSM；间接受益 AMAT、KLAC、LRCX、ASML、AMKR、ASE，以及 hybrid bonding/先进封装设备链。
  • 为什么重要：没有 CoWoS/2.5D 封装，GPU die 和 HBM 不能变成可交付 AI accelerator。Nextwaves 指出 AI chip 瓶颈从 wafer supply 转向 CoWoS advanced packaging，TSMC CoWoS 2026 仍紧，并提到 custom ASIC 占 CoWoS accelerator shipments 的比例上升。来源：Nextwaves。
  • 继续上涨的条件：TSMC CoWoS-L/CoWoS-S 维持高利用率；Nvidia、AMD、Broadcom、Marvell 与云厂商提前锁定 2026/2027 封装产能；chiplet 和 HBM 更深度集成提高封装价值量。
  • 重点观察：TSMC capex 中 advanced packaging 比重；CoWoS 月产能、利用率和交期；OSAT 外包扩产；KLA/AMAT/LRCX 先进封装、process control、test 相关订单；良率改善速度。
  • 主要风险：若先进制程 wafer 反而成为新瓶颈，CoWoS 扩产可能短期过剩；TSMC 垂直整合挤压 OSAT 利润；台湾地缘风险。

方向 5：foundry 与先进制程 N3/N2

  • 代表公司：TSM，潜在观察 INTC foundry 进展。
  • 为什么重要：Nvidia、AMD、Broadcom、Marvell、Apple 和多数 hyperscaler ASIC 都依赖 TSMC leading-edge。SemiAnalysis 近期讨论认为，随着 N3/N2 需求集中到 AI accelerator，前端 advanced logic capacity 也可能成为新瓶颈。来源：SemiAnalysis。
  • 继续上涨的条件：N3/N2 产能持续满载；AI/HPC revenue mix 提升；客户愿意为 hot lot、先进封装和领先节点支付溢价；海外 fab 不显著稀释毛利。
  • 重点观察：TSMC HPC revenue 占比；N3/N2 utilization 和 lead time；2nm 良率/量产节奏；capex guidance；gross margin 是否能抵消海外 fab 成本。
  • 主要风险：客户集中度上升；capex air pocket；台湾风险；Intel/Samsung foundry 若恢复竞争会压低溢价。

方向 6：AI networking、以太网 fabric 与光互连

  • 代表公司：ANET、AVGO、MRVL、COHR、LITE、CRDO，NVDA networking 也是核心。
  • 为什么重要：AI cluster 规模扩大后，瓶颈从单卡算力转向 east-west traffic、scale-up/scale-out bandwidth 和延迟。Deloitte 预计 CPO、LPO 等光互连在 2026 年更重要，并称 AI network fabric spending 2024-2029 CAGR 预计达 38%。来源：Deloitte。
  • 继续上涨的条件：800G 进入主流，1.6T 开始放量；CPO/silicon photonics 从展示走向生产；以太网 AI fabric 获得更多 hyperscaler 采用；NVLink 与以太网双路线都扩大。
  • 重点观察：Arista AI networking revenue、客户集中度和 800G/1.6T 节奏；Broadcom Tomahawk/Jericho、Marvell DSP/optical interconnect 订单；光模块价格和交期；CPO/LPO 标准化进展。
  • 主要风险：客户建设节奏波动；光模块价格竞争；技术路线不确定；若电力或封装限制 GPU 部署，网络订单也会延后。

方向 7：电源管理、供电链、液冷与数据中心基础设施

  • 代表公司：VRT、ETN、GEV、MPWR，部分服务器/ODM 链如 SMCI 需单独评估。
  • 为什么重要：AI 机柜功率密度上升后，约束从“有没有芯片”转向“有没有电、能不能散热、机房能不能按时交付”。Creative Strategies 明确提出约束正从 compute 迁移到 energy。来源：Creative Strategies。
  • 继续上涨的条件：液冷 CDU、UPS、switchgear、变压器、高压直流供电和电网接入 backlog 持续上修；AI data center 项目从预订进入交付。
  • 重点观察：Vertiv/Eaton/GE Vernova backlog 与 book-to-bill；数据中心 interconnection queue；PPA/电力采购协议；液冷渗透率；单 rack 功率密度。
  • 主要风险：不是纯半导体，估值可能已反映 AI 外溢；项目审批、电网、环保和社区阻力会拉长交付；客户延迟建设会造成订单递延。

方向 8：半导体设备、测试、process control 与 EDA/IP

  • 代表公司：ASML、AMAT、LRCX、KLAC、TER、SNPS、CDNS。
  • 为什么重要：这是 AI capex 的“卖铲人”层。设备受益于先进逻辑、HBM、先进封装、测试复杂度提升；EDA/IP 受益于 chiplet、custom ASIC、多 die system 和先进节点设计复杂度。
  • 继续上涨的条件：WFE 上修；先进封装/test/process control 订单快于传统前端；EDA backlog 与 AI/custom silicon 项目同步增加；客户不削减 2027 capex。
  • 重点观察：ASML backlog、EUV/High-NA 订单；AMAT/LRCX/KLAC 先进封装、HBM、process control 收入；TER 测试需求；SNPS/CDNS revenue backlog、design starts、AI/ASIC 相关 IP demand。
  • 主要风险：设备订单是 capex 周期的放大器，转弱时跌得早；China exposure 受出口管制影响；EDA 估值高、增长较平滑但弹性不如芯片。

方向 9：edge/on-device AI、PC/手机/汽车/工业 AI

  • 代表公司：QCOM、AMD、INTC、NVDA、ON、ADI、TXN，部分汽车芯片和 MCU/模拟链。
  • 为什么重要：长期看，AI 不会只在云端。IDC 提到 AI 正扩散到 edge deployments 和 client devices；Deloitte 也认为推理负载扩大后，端侧与高带宽 flash/存储会更重要。来源：IDC、Deloitte。
  • 继续上涨的条件：AI PC/AI phone 从营销卖点变成换机驱动；端侧模型需要更高内存、更强 NPU 和更好功耗；汽车/工业 AI 恢复增长。
  • 重点观察：AI PC 渗透率、32GB+ memory attach rate、手机端侧 AI 使用率、Qualcomm/AMD/Intel NPU 平台采用、汽车半导体库存消化。
  • 主要风险：目前端侧 AI 货币化弱于云端；PC/手机周期仍偏消费电子；汽车/工业芯片有库存和宏观周期压力。

六、交叉验证后的结论

高置信度结论：AI capex、HBM、先进封装和数据中心网络确实是 2026 年半导体行业的核心变量。IDC、Deloitte、Nvidia、AMD、Micron、TSMC 相关资料在方向上相互印证。

中等置信度结论：AI 半导体受益面会从 Nvidia 扩散到 ASIC、networking、memory、equipment、EDA、power/thermal。但不同公司的弹性和风险不一样，不能把“AI 受益”当成统一标签。

低置信度或争议结论：2026 年后的需求能否持续、云厂商 AI ROI 是否足够高、HBM 是否结构性摆脱周期、GPU 折旧是否合理。这些问题目前没有单一数据能证明，只能靠未来几个季度的 capex、云收入、毛利、价格和利用率共同验证。

我的最终判断是：美股半导体 AI 方向仍有继续上涨的基本面信号，但已经过了“只要有 AI 标签就涨”的阶段。下一阶段更可能是分化：真正卡住瓶颈、订单可见度强、能把 capex 转化为利润的公司继续受益；只靠叙事、估值提前透支、或暴露于供给反转的公司会被市场惩罚。

Deep Survey of the US AI Semiconductor Theme: Signals For Further Upside And The Bear Case

Date: 2026-05-14

Note: This article is industry and market research, not investment advice. Because some PDF/IR pages can only be fetched as binary or JS pages in the current environment, this article prioritizes directly accessible company announcements, industry research pages, Tavily/Web search summaries, and cross-validation from parallel research agents. Single-source or disputed estimates are explicitly marked.

Core Conclusion

The US semiconductor AI trade has moved from "buy GPUs" to "buy AI infrastructure bottlenecks". If the theme continues to rise, the strongest signals will probably not come only from Nvidia, but from a verifiable chain: hyperscalers keep raising capex, GPU/ASIC orders remain constrained by advanced process and packaging, HBM and DRAM/NAND prices stay strong, and networking interconnect, power/cooling, testing, and yield control become the next shortages.

But the bear case has also become more specific. It is not simply saying "AI is a bubble". It asks four questions: can cloud providers earn back this round of capital spending? Is the true economic life of GPUs shorter than accounting depreciation? Will HBM/packaging expansion come back to pressure prices after 2027? Has the market already priced in high growth, high margins, high share, and a low-rate environment all at once?

So the current stage looks more like "fundamentals are still strong, but the margin for error has fallen". The most important things to track are not one-day stock moves, but whether orders, capex, prices, yields, gross margin, and cloud AI revenue disclosures continue to confirm one another.

I. Why AI Semiconductors Still Have Industry Signals For Further Upside

1. AI capex remains the source variable for the entire chain

In its April 2026 semiconductor outlook, IDC forecast that global semiconductor revenue will reach $1.29 trillion in 2026, up 52.8% year over year, and said AI infrastructure has become the structurally dominant source of demand. Its summary also said hyperscale capex surpassed $100 billion for the first time in 2025 Q3, and that 2026 i4 capex is expected to grow about 70% year over year, approaching $600 billion. Source: IDC.

MUFG's AI financing weekly gives a similar direction, saying 2026 Big Five hyperscaler capex is expected to exceed $600 billion, of which about 75%, or $450 billion, is directly related to AI infrastructure. Source: MUFG PDF.

The significance of this signal is that semiconductor revenue is the upstream mirror of cloud-provider capital spending. As long as Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Oracle, and others keep expanding AI data centers, GPU, custom ASIC, HBM, advanced packaging, switches, optical interconnect, power equipment, and test equipment all still have demand support. Conversely, if these companies' capex guidance is revised down repeatedly, the valuation foundation for AI semiconductors will shake quickly.

2. Nvidia is still proving through revenue and gross margin that demand has not cooled clearly

Nvidia's FY2026 Q4 announcement showed quarterly revenue of $68.1 billion, up 73% year over year; data center revenue of $62.3 billion, up 75% year over year; and full-year revenue of $215.9 billion, up 65%. The company also disclosed Q4 GAAP gross margin of 75.0%. Source: Nvidia SEC 8-K / Q4 FY2026 release.

Jensen Huang said in the same announcement:

"Computing demand is growing exponentially, the agentic AI inflection point has arrived."

This type of management statement is naturally sales-oriented. But revenue, data center growth, and high gross margin together show that, at least through early 2026, the market had not yet seen the typical semiconductor downcycle combination of prices loosening first, gross margin falling first, and order visibility declining.

The positive signal for further upside is that Nvidia's networking revenue, system-level revenue, and next-generation Rubin platform visibility continue to strengthen, rather than the story depending only on a single GPU ASP. The bear side should watch whether gross margin falls meaningfully from the 70%+ range, and whether customers start pushing for price cuts after supply-chain expansion.

3. AI is not only Nvidia: custom ASIC and inference economics may broaden the beneficiary base

AMD's 2026 Q1 announcement said first-quarter performance was driven by accelerating AI infrastructure demand, and that Data Center has become the main driver of revenue and profit growth. The company guided 2026 Q2 revenue to a midpoint of $11.2 billion, up about 46% year over year, with non-GAAP gross margin around 56%. Lisa Su also mentioned that inferencing and agentic AI are driving CPU and accelerator demand, and that customer forecasts for the MI450 Series and Helios exceeded initial expectations. Source: AMD Q1 2026 results.

This means the second stage of AI semiconductors may move from "training GPU shortage" to "training + inference + agentic workload + custom chips". This is also the logic for custom silicon / networking suppliers such as Broadcom and Marvell: when inference cost becomes the core variable, cloud providers are more willing to use ASICs to optimize specific workloads.

This is a double-edged sword for the industry. It is a broadening tailwind for Broadcom, Marvell, TSMC, and EDA/IP. For Nvidia, it is a potential long-term pressure on share and gross margin. The best bull signal would be ASIC ramping without crushing GPUs, and instead expanding the AI compute TAM together.

4. HBM and memory may be among the hardest bottlenecks in 2026

IDC describes memory as central to this cycle. It expects 2026 DRAM revenue to reach $418.6 billion, with significant growth driven by HBM and AI infrastructure, and says HBM has become one of the main constraints in the AI accelerator supply chain. Source: IDC.

Deloitte's 2026 semiconductor outlook also emphasizes that AI training and inference demand for HBM3, HBM4, and DDR7 has caused shortages in other consumer memory. Some DDR4/DDR5 prices rose about fourfold between September and November 2025, and Deloitte warns that prices may continue to rise in the first half of 2026. Source: Deloitte 2026 Semiconductor Industry Outlook.

Micron's investor materials give more direct company-level signals: AI demand is driving DRAM and NAND data center bit TAM above 50% of industry TAM for the first time in 2026. The company says both AI and traditional server demand are constrained by insufficient DRAM/NAND supply, and expects FY2026 capex to exceed $25 billion. Source: Micron investor material.

HBM is special because it is not simply a price-up version of a normal memory cycle. It requires high-yield stacking, advanced packaging, long customer qualification, and it crowds out standard DRAM wafer capacity. If the bull case holds, companies like Micron may be re-rated as "critical AI bottleneck suppliers", not traditional highly cyclical memory stocks.

5. Advanced packaging, testing, and yield control have moved from backend supporting roles into the main story

The first Tavily pass and multiple external searches point to the same issue: AI chip limits are not only in wafer front-end supply, but also in CoWoS, advanced packaging, HBM stacking, testing, and yield control. KLA-related earnings summaries show that the company gained share in advanced wafer-level packaging and expects advanced packaging process-control revenue to rise from about $635 million in 2025 to about $1 billion in 2026. Source: Yahoo Finance / KLA Q3 2026 earnings summary.

TSMC's 2026 capex plan also provides indirect validation. Bloomberg reported that TSMC plans 2026 capex of $52 billion to $56 billion, and expects 2026 revenue growth of nearly 30%, which the market interpreted as evidence of sustained strong AI demand. Source: Bloomberg summary page.

Companies worth watching on this chain include TSM, ASML, AMAT, LRCX, KLAC, Teradyne, and advanced packaging OSAT/materials/testing companies. The signal for further upside is not a line like "AI is hot", but whether advanced packaging capacity remains booked, equipment order backlogs lengthen, and yield-tool revenue accelerates.

6. Networking, optical interconnect, power, and cooling have become the new AI data center constraints

Nvidia's Q4 FY2026 announcement split data center revenue into compute and networking. TechCrunch's coverage of its earnings said roughly $11 billion of quarterly data center revenue came from networking products such as NVLink. Source: TechCrunch.

This shows that an AI cluster is not just "buy more GPUs". Large-scale inference and training require low-latency networks, switches, optical modules, power management, liquid cooling, data center power, and data center construction. If the market shifts from chip picker to infrastructure basket, ANET, MRVL, AVGO, MPWR, VRT, and others may continue to benefit.

Two signals are needed to verify this chain: first, whether networking revenue grows faster than the traditional enterprise networking cycle; second, whether power/cooling company backlogs come from AI data centers rather than a normal industrial cycle.

II. Watchlist For Future Upside

To judge whether the US semiconductor AI theme has further upside, I would split the signals into three layers by strength.

The first layer is hard demand signals: hyperscaler capex guidance revised up or not revised down; Nvidia/AMD/Broadcom/Marvell order visibility lengthening; HBM supply from Micron/SK hynix/Samsung locked in ahead of time; TSMC advanced node and CoWoS capacity still in shortage.

The second layer is pricing and profit signals: Nvidia data center gross margin staying high; Micron HBM and DRAM/NAND pricing continuing to improve; equipment company orders and service revenue strengthening together; networking and power equipment company backlogs becoming higher quality.

The third layer is diffusion signals: AI revenue no longer concentrated only in Nvidia, but diffusing into ASIC, EDA, packaging, testing, memory, networking, power, and cooling. The healthier the diffusion, the less the sector looks like a single-leader trade. If diffusion is only low-quality catch-up buying, risk rises instead.

III. The Bear Case: Why This Rally Could Go Wrong

1. Valuation's margin for error has fallen

The stock prices of core AI semiconductor companies no longer reflect only "good growth this year". They reflect the maintenance of high growth, high share, and high gross margin for many future years. The bear side's strongest point is that even if fundamentals do not collapse, if growth falls from extremely fast to "still very good but below expectations", valuation multiples can compress.

This risk is most typical in Nvidia, but it also spreads to Broadcom, AMD, Micron, TSMC, ASML, and others. Bulls can argue that earnings growth will digest valuation. Bears will ask whether current prices have already bought the optimistic 2027-2028 scenario in advance.

2. Cloud-provider ROI remains opaque

J.P. Morgan Asset Management's 2026 outlook lists the hyperscaler AI investment "metaverse moment" as an important risk: the four major hyperscalers have spent about $1.3 trillion on capex and R&D since 2022 Q4, but whether AI investment can produce commensurate profits still needs verification. The report also mentions that declining free cash flow margins and lower cash balances make a clearer profitability path more necessary in 2026. Source: J.P. Morgan Asset Management PDF.

This is the real key bear case: semiconductor companies recognize revenue today, while cloud providers bear future depreciation, power, and utilization risk. If AI application revenue cannot cover the cost of capital, chip orders may switch from "the more the better" to "digest inventory and capacity first".

3. Depreciation and GPU economic life may pressure profits

Some skeptics argue that AI GPU technology iterates too quickly, so true economic life may be shorter than cloud providers' accounting depreciation periods. If 5-6 year depreciation assumptions are too optimistic, cloud providers' current profits may understate future replacement, impairment, and idle-capacity risk.

The bull rebuttal is that older GPUs still have high utilization in inference, internal workloads, lower-end models, and the cloud rental market, and that token demand is large enough to allow different generations of equipment to be used in tiers. If future used GPU prices, cloud GPU rental prices, and utilization all stay firm, the depreciation bear case will weaken.

4. HBM and memory still cannot escape the supply cycle

HBM is scarce today, but that does not mean it will be scarce forever. Micron, SK hynix, and Samsung are all expanding HBM and cleanroom investment. Deloitte also warns that if DRAM/NAND capex rises further because of higher prices, it may create oversupply again. Source: Deloitte.

Micron's low forward P/E looks cheap, but memory stocks often look cheapest near cycle peaks because the market expects peak earnings to be unsustainable. What the bear side should watch is not how high 2026 EPS is, but whether prices can hold after 2027, once HBM4 competition, Samsung qualification, and standard DRAM/NAND supply recovery arrive.

5. Custom ASIC is a diffusion opportunity, and also Nvidia's long-term substitution risk

The rise of ASIC can broaden the AI semiconductor beneficiary base, but it may also change profit distribution. Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, in-house inference ASICs, Broadcom custom chips, and Marvell custom interconnect solutions all shift some value from general-purpose GPUs toward customers and the custom silicon supply chain.

In the short term, this may not hurt Nvidia because the GPU ecosystem, CUDA, NVLink, system integration, and time-to-market remain strong. But over the long term, if inference workloads standardize, model architectures stabilize, and customers put more weight on cost per token, ASIC pressure on GPU gross margin and share will grow.

6. China restrictions, Taiwan risk, and supply-chain concentration remain meaningful tail risks

In its Q4 FY2026 announcement, Nvidia noted that although a small number of its China H200 products had been approved, they had not yet generated revenue, and the company was uncertain whether the related products would be allowed to be imported into China. Source: Nvidia SEC 8-K / Q4 FY2026 release.

At the same time, TSMC's concentrated position in leading-edge processes and advanced packaging exposes the entire AI semiconductor chain to Taiwan geopolitical risk. US export controls may limit China revenue, but they may also push China to accelerate domestic substitution. This risk is hard to price in normal times, but if it erupts, the whole chain would be re-rated quickly.

IV. Future Sector Map: The Opportunity Is Not Only In GPUs, But In Bottleneck Migration

More systematically, the future main line for AI semiconductors is not one direction, but "bottleneck migration": first not enough GPUs, then not enough HBM, not enough CoWoS/advanced packaging, not enough N3/N2 advanced process, not enough network bandwidth, not enough power and cooling. Capital often chases the most constrained link at the time, instead of staying fixed in one type of company.

Omdia's 2026 semiconductor trends list data center servers as the dominant driver of semiconductor revenue, explicitly mentioning that GPU, logic ASSP/ASIC, HBM, and power management IC are all driven by AI data centers. It also says 2026 themes include larger-scale training, continued CSP expansion, old server upgrades, power/cooling improvements, and edge AI growth. Source: Omdia summary.

TrendForce's AI server outlook also focuses 2026 on hyperscaler capex, NVIDIA rack-scale platforms, US CSP in-house ASICs, tight HBM/CoWoS, liquid cooling going mainstream, and localization caused by US-China restrictions. Source: TrendForce.

Direction 1: Training GPUs / general-purpose AI acceleration platforms

  • Representative companies: NVDA, AMD.
  • Why it matters: frontier model training, post-training, reinforcement learning, long context, and agentic AI still require high-end GPU clusters. Nvidia today is not only GPUs, but a platform of GPU + NVLink/NVSwitch + networking + rack-scale system + CUDA.
  • Conditions for further upside: Blackwell/Rubin shipments continue to beat expectations; inference does not weaken GPU demand, and instead expands total compute through token consumption growth; data center gross margin stays high; networking and system revenue mix rises.
  • Key things to watch: Nvidia data center revenue/gross margin; Blackwell/Rubin lead times; cloud GPU rental prices and utilization; AMD MI series customer count, revenue, and gross margin; CUDA/ROCm software ecosystem progress.
  • Main risks: valuation is the most fully priced; gross margin is sensitive to supply release and customer price pressure; ASIC replaces some GPU demand in predictable inference workloads; export controls affect China revenue.

Direction 2: Inference accelerators and custom ASIC

  • Representative companies: AVGO, MRVL, AMD, with indirect benefit to TSM. Cloud in-house chips include Google TPU, Amazon Trainium/Inferentia, Microsoft Maia, and Meta MTIA.
  • Why it matters: as AI moves from training to large-scale inference, the key metric shifts from "absolute performance" to "cost per token, performance per watt, deployable scale". Creative Strategies breaks the 2026 framework into capex sustainability, constraint migration from compute to energy, inference economics, in-house silicon expansion, and memory/packaging physical bottlenecks. Source: Creative Strategies.
  • Conditions for further upside: Broadcom/Marvell custom silicon backlog revised up; cloud-provider TPU/Trainium/Maia/MTIA move from internal experiments to large-scale deployment; ASIC and GPU are incremental capacity expansion rather than zero-sum substitution.
  • Key things to watch: AVGO/MRVL AI revenue or custom silicon backlog; cloud-provider in-house chip generation launches and deployment scale; HBM/CoWoS allocation used by ASIC; whether API call volume grows faster after inference API costs fall.
  • Main risks: project-based revenue is concentrated among a few hyperscalers; in-house chips may let cloud providers keep the profits; if ASIC mainly replaces GPU instead of expanding the market, it will compress NVDA valuation.

Direction 3: HBM, DRAM, NAND, and the AI storage layer

  • Representative companies: MU; overseas counterparts SK hynix and Samsung.
  • Why it matters: HBM is a hard bottleneck for training and high-end inference. Long context, agentic workflow, checkpointing, RAG, and multimodal data will also drive DRAM/NAND/enterprise SSD. IDC expects 2026 DRAM revenue to rise sharply because of HBM and DDR demand, and says HBM capacity is mostly booked through 2026, with some extending into 2027. Source: IDC.
  • Conditions for further upside: HBM4/HBM4E qualification goes smoothly; long-term supply agreements, prepayments, and price floors increase; HBM crowds out standard DRAM wafer capacity, also tightening ordinary DRAM/NAND.
  • Key things to watch: Micron HBM revenue/share, HBM4 mass production and Nvidia/AMD qualification; HBM ASP and contract duration; DRAM/NAND spot and contract prices; enterprise SSD prices and supply; whether capex moves from "disciplined expansion" to "capacity scramble".
  • Main risks: memory remains a highly cyclical industry; Samsung ramp or customer inventory buildup may cause prices to fall; high capex creates oversupply after 2027.

Direction 4: Advanced packaging, CoWoS, chiplets, and 2.5D/3D integration

  • Representative companies: TSM; indirect beneficiaries AMAT, KLAC, LRCX, ASML, AMKR, ASE, and the hybrid bonding/advanced packaging equipment chain.
  • Why it matters: without CoWoS/2.5D packaging, GPU die and HBM cannot become deliverable AI accelerators. Nextwaves says the AI chip bottleneck has shifted from wafer supply to CoWoS advanced packaging, that TSMC CoWoS remains tight in 2026, and that custom ASIC's share of CoWoS accelerator shipments is rising. Source: Nextwaves.
  • Conditions for further upside: TSMC CoWoS-L/CoWoS-S maintain high utilization; Nvidia, AMD, Broadcom, Marvell, and cloud providers lock in 2026/2027 packaging capacity early; deeper chiplet and HBM integration raises packaging content value.
  • Key things to watch: advanced packaging share of TSMC capex; CoWoS monthly capacity, utilization, and lead times; OSAT outsourced expansion; advanced packaging, process control, and test-related orders at KLA/AMAT/LRCX; pace of yield improvement.
  • Main risks: if advanced process wafers instead become the new bottleneck, CoWoS expansion may briefly overshoot; TSMC vertical integration squeezes OSAT margins; Taiwan geopolitical risk.

Direction 5: Foundry and advanced process N3/N2

  • Representative companies: TSM, with INTC foundry progress worth monitoring.
  • Why it matters: Nvidia, AMD, Broadcom, Marvell, Apple, and most hyperscaler ASICs all depend on TSMC leading-edge. Recent SemiAnalysis discussion argues that as N3/N2 demand concentrates in AI accelerators, front-end advanced logic capacity may also become a new bottleneck. Source: SemiAnalysis.
  • Conditions for further upside: N3/N2 capacity remains fully loaded; AI/HPC revenue mix rises; customers are willing to pay premiums for hot lots, advanced packaging, and leading nodes; overseas fabs do not materially dilute gross margin.
  • Key things to watch: TSMC HPC revenue share; N3/N2 utilization and lead time; 2nm yield/mass production pace; capex guidance; whether gross margin can offset overseas fab costs.
  • Main risks: rising customer concentration; capex air pocket; Taiwan risk; Intel/Samsung foundry recovering competitiveness would reduce premiums.

Direction 6: AI networking, Ethernet fabric, and optical interconnect

  • Representative companies: ANET, AVGO, MRVL, COHR, LITE, CRDO. NVDA networking is also core.
  • Why it matters: after AI clusters scale, the bottleneck shifts from single-card compute to east-west traffic, scale-up/scale-out bandwidth, and latency. Deloitte expects optical interconnect such as CPO and LPO to become more important in 2026, and says AI network fabric spending is expected to grow at a 38% CAGR from 2024 to 2029. Source: Deloitte.
  • Conditions for further upside: 800G enters the mainstream and 1.6T starts ramping; CPO/silicon photonics move from demos to production; Ethernet AI fabric gains more hyperscaler adoption; both NVLink and Ethernet routes expand.
  • Key things to watch: Arista AI networking revenue, customer concentration, and 800G/1.6T cadence; Broadcom Tomahawk/Jericho and Marvell DSP/optical interconnect orders; optical module prices and lead times; CPO/LPO standardization progress.
  • Main risks: customer buildout cadence fluctuates; optical module price competition; uncertain technical route; if power or packaging limits GPU deployment, networking orders will also be delayed.

Direction 7: Power management, power delivery chain, liquid cooling, and data center infrastructure

  • Representative companies: VRT, ETN, GEV, MPWR. Some server/ODM chain companies such as SMCI need separate evaluation.
  • Why it matters: after AI rack power density rises, the constraint shifts from "are there chips" to "is there power, can it be cooled, can the data center be delivered on time". Creative Strategies explicitly says the constraint is migrating from compute to energy. Source: Creative Strategies.
  • Conditions for further upside: liquid cooling CDU, UPS, switchgear, transformers, high-voltage DC power delivery, and grid interconnection backlogs continue to be revised up; AI data center projects move from reservations into delivery.
  • Key things to watch: Vertiv/Eaton/GE Vernova backlog and book-to-bill; data center interconnection queue; PPAs/power purchase agreements; liquid cooling penetration; power density per rack.
  • Main risks: these are not pure semiconductors, and valuations may already reflect AI spillover; project approval, grid, environmental, and community resistance can lengthen delivery; customer construction delays can defer orders.

Direction 8: Semiconductor equipment, testing, process control, and EDA/IP

  • Representative companies: ASML, AMAT, LRCX, KLAC, TER, SNPS, CDNS.
  • Why it matters: this is the "picks and shovels" layer of AI capex. Equipment benefits from advanced logic, HBM, advanced packaging, and higher test complexity; EDA/IP benefits from chiplets, custom ASIC, multi-die systems, and higher design complexity at advanced nodes.
  • Conditions for further upside: WFE is revised up; advanced packaging/test/process-control orders grow faster than traditional front-end; EDA backlog increases together with AI/custom silicon projects; customers do not cut 2027 capex.
  • Key things to watch: ASML backlog and EUV/High-NA orders; AMAT/LRCX/KLAC advanced packaging, HBM, and process-control revenue; TER test demand; SNPS/CDNS revenue backlog, design starts, and AI/ASIC-related IP demand.
  • Main risks: equipment orders amplify the capex cycle and fall early when the cycle weakens; China exposure is affected by export controls; EDA valuations are high, growth is smoother but less elastic than chips.

Direction 9: Edge/on-device AI, PC/phone/auto/industrial AI

  • Representative companies: QCOM, AMD, INTC, NVDA, ON, ADI, TXN, and parts of the auto chip and MCU/analog chain.
  • Why it matters: over the long term, AI will not stay only in the cloud. IDC mentions AI spreading into edge deployments and client devices. Deloitte also believes that after inference workloads expand, on-device and high-bandwidth flash/storage will become more important. Sources: IDC, Deloitte.
  • Conditions for further upside: AI PC/AI phone move from marketing points into replacement-cycle drivers; on-device models require more memory, stronger NPUs, and better power consumption; auto/industrial AI returns to growth.
  • Key things to watch: AI PC penetration, 32GB+ memory attach rate, on-phone AI usage, Qualcomm/AMD/Intel NPU platform adoption, auto semiconductor inventory digestion.
  • Main risks: on-device AI monetization is currently weaker than cloud; PC/phone cycles still lean consumer electronics; auto/industrial chips face inventory and macro-cycle pressure.

A practical judgment: if capex, HBM, CoWoS, GPU rental, AI networking, and power backlog all strengthen at the same time, AI semiconductors are still in a "multi-bottleneck resonance" phase. If Nvidia revenue is still strong, but HBM/CoWoS/rental prices/cloud capex have already weakened, that is usually a signal that risk is moving forward.

VI. Cross-Validated Conclusion

High-confidence conclusion: AI capex, HBM, advanced packaging, and data center networking are indeed the core variables for the semiconductor industry in 2026. IDC, Deloitte, Nvidia, AMD, Micron, and TSMC-related materials confirm one another directionally.

Medium-confidence conclusion: AI semiconductor beneficiaries will broaden from Nvidia into ASIC, networking, memory, equipment, EDA, power/thermal. But different companies have different elasticity and risk, so "AI beneficiary" should not be treated as one uniform label.

Low-confidence or disputed conclusions: whether demand can persist after 2026, whether cloud-provider AI ROI is high enough, whether HBM has structurally escaped cyclicality, and whether GPU depreciation assumptions are reasonable. No single data point can prove these questions right now. They can only be validated through capex, cloud revenue, margins, prices, and utilization over the next several quarters.

My final judgment is: the US semiconductor AI theme still has fundamental signals for further upside, but it has moved past the stage where anything with an AI label goes up. The next stage is more likely to be divergent: companies that truly sit on bottlenecks, have strong order visibility, and can turn capex into profit will keep benefiting; companies relying only on narrative, already overdrawn valuation, or exposure to supply reversal will be punished by the market.
