Snowflake (SNOW) Q1 FY2027 财报与盘后上涨调研

日期：2026-05-27
主题：Snowflake 今日财报、盘后股价、上涨原因与预期差

核心结论

Snowflake 盘后大涨的直接原因不是单一 EPS beat，而是三件事叠加：收入/产品收入明显超预期、全年产品收入指引从 27% 增长上调到 31% 增长、AI 产品使用量开始被市场视为能转化成实际消费收入。市场原本担心 Snowflake 增长降速、AI 投入拖累毛利和现金流；这次结果给出的是相反信号：增长再加速，同时非 GAAP 经营利润率指引还被上调。

盘后价格不同来源在不同时间点有差异。MarketBeat 在 4:51pm ET 显示 SNOW 盘后报 233.59 美元，较常规收盘价 174.60 美元上涨 58.99 美元，涨幅 33.79%；Google Finance 在 4:52pm ET 显示盘后 235.20 美元，涨 59.94 美元，涨幅 34.20%；CNBC 较早时点 4:06pm ET 显示盘后 215.92 美元，涨 40.66 美元，涨幅 23.20%；IBD 报道称盘后涨超 30% 至 231.20 美元附近。盘后流动性较低，价格快速跳动，本文采用“约 +23% 到 +34%，后续稳定在 +30% 左右”作为更稳妥描述。来源：MarketBeat、Google Finance、CNBC、IBD。

财报本身：超预期在哪里

Snowflake 官方财报通过 IR/Business Wire/8-K 发布，Q1 FY2027 截至 2026-04-30。官方 IR 稿和 StockTitan 转录稿显示：产品收入 13.34 亿美元，同比增长 34%；总收入 13.91 亿美元，同比增长 33%；净收入留存率 126%；RPO 92.1 亿美元，同比增长 38%；年化产品收入超过 100 万美元的客户数 779 个，同比增长 29%。来源：Snowflake IR 官方稿、StockTitan 官方稿转载、StockTitan 8-K 页面。

官方管理层措辞很强。CEO Sridhar Ramaswamy 说：

“Snowflake delivered a milestone quarter, with product revenue of $1.33 billion, up 34% year-over-year, marking the strongest sequential dollar growth in our history... AI continues to be a powerful tailwind for Snowflake, and Q1 marks a clear inflection point in that journey.”

CFO Brian Robins 也把上调全年指引直接归因于核心平台和 AI 业务动能：

“Given the strong momentum across both our core platform business and AI business, we are raising our full-year product revenue guidance.”

与市场预期对比，IBD 报道称调整后 EPS 为 0.39 美元，华尔街预期 0.32 美元；总收入 13.9 亿美元，预期 13.2 亿美元。MarketBeat 的财报页给出同样口径：Actual EPS 0.39 美元，Consensus EPS 0.32 美元，beat 0.07 美元；Actual Revenue 13.9 亿美元，Expected Revenue 13.2 亿美元，beat 约 6714 万美元。来源：IBD、MarketBeat。

指引：真正点燃盘后的部分

财报后的股价反应主要来自 forward guide。Snowflake 对 Q2 FY2027 的产品收入指引为 14.15 亿至 14.20 亿美元，IBD 称高于市场共识 13.74 亿美元；全年 FY2027 产品收入指引上调至 58.40 亿美元，同比增长 31%，高于此前 56.60 亿美元、同比增长 27% 的指引。来源：IBD、官方稿转载。

这一点重要，因为财报前市场关注的不是“能不能小幅 beat”，而是 AI 是否真的推动消费增长、全年 27% 增长是否偏保守。TIKR 在财报前写得很直接：

“The market will not react to a number that simply matches guidance. The real tells are RPO trajectory and full-year guidance... A full-year guidance raise would signal that Cortex Code and Snowflake Intelligence are pulling consumption above the baseline ahead of schedule.”

来源：TIKR 财报前分析。实际结果正好击中这个 bull case：不仅 Q1 超预期，而且全年产品收入指引从 27% 拉到 31%。

为什么会上涨

第一，产品收入超预期。Snowflake 的商业模式是 consumption-based，产品收入比总收入更直接反映客户实际使用量。官方稿解释 product revenue 主要来自客户对 compute、storage、data transfer 的消费，不是传统订阅软件按合约期线性确认收入。因此 Q1 产品收入 13.34 亿美元、同比 34%，比此前 Q1 产品收入指引 12.62 亿至 12.67 亿美元强很多。此前 Q1 指引来自 Q4 FY2026 官方 8-K：Q1 FY2027 产品收入 12.62 亿至 12.67 亿美元，同比增长 27%；全年 56.60 亿美元，同比增长 27%。来源：SEC Q4 FY2026 8-K/财报稿。

第二，全年指引上修改变了增长叙事。财报前熊方叙事是增长从 FY2026 的约 29% 降到中 20%，AI 还是 narrative 而非 revenue。AInvest 财报前写道，市场真正问题是估值压缩是否过度，bull case 是 Q1 revenue beat 并上调全年指引、FY2027 增长保持在 28% 以上；bear case 是收入接近或低于 13.2 亿美元、指引下调或增长落入 low-20s。来源：AInvest 财报前分析。实际结果是 31% 全年产品收入增长指引，明显落在 bull case。

第三，AI 产品开始被视为“带动消费”的证据，而不只是发布会故事。官方稿称超过 13,600 个账户正在使用 Snowflake AI capabilities，Snowflake Intelligence 账户数环比翻倍，Cortex Code 已被超过 7,100 个账户使用；这些创新“contributed to the strongest sequential product revenue dollar growth in company history”。来源：StockTitan 8-K 页面。这正对应财报前市场疑问：AI 工具是否能转化为更重的平台消费，而不只是产品 adoption 指标。

第四，盈利质量没有被 AI 投入拖垮。官方稿显示 Q1 非 GAAP operating income 为 1.658 亿美元，margin 11.9%；adjusted free cash flow 为 2.655 亿美元，margin 19.1%。全年非 GAAP operating margin 指引上调到 13.5%，此前为 12.5%。这缓解了“AI 产品毛利更低、Observe 收购拖累 FCF”的担忧。来源：官方稿转载。

为什么“出乎意料”

出乎意料的核心不是“市场完全没想到会 beat”。财报前有不少 bullish checks，甚至有人预期 Snowflake 会超过指引。真正超出的是幅度和质量：产品收入从此前指引的 12.62-12.67 亿美元跳到 13.34 亿美元，全年产品收入增长指引从 27% 提到 31%，同时经营利润率指引也上调。

更准确地说，市场并不是今天才把 AI 视为 Snowflake 的增长信号。AI 受益早已是共识，甚至财报前已经反映在不少 bullish 观点里。CRN 在 Q4 后写到 Snowflake “enters a new fiscal year on more stable growth footing, with some notable successes in its artificial intelligence product portfolio”，并提到 Wall Street 对 FY2027 产品收入的预期约 55.5 亿美元，而 Snowflake 当时指引约 56.6 亿美元；Bank of America 甚至认为 Snowflake 可以达到至少 30% 同比增长。来源：CRN。Reuters 2 月报道的标题也是 “as AI boosts demand”，说明 AI 拉动需求这件事已经不是全新叙事。来源：The Globe and Mail/Reuters。

真正没有完全成为共识的是“AI adoption 能否变成可持续、可预测、且不牺牲利润率的 consumption revenue”。这也是为什么财报前的讨论重点不是 Snowflake 有没有 AI，而是 AI 是否 monetizes。Barchart 财报前文章标题直接是 “Snowflake: The Market Wants Proof That AI Products Actually Monetize”，并指出投资者会审视 Snowflake 的 agentic AI capabilities 和 enterprise governance platform 是否能转化成 durable, long-term growth。来源：Barchart。TIKR 也把问题说得很窄：市场不会对 merely matching guidance 有反应，真正的信号是 RPO trajectory 和 full-year guidance；如果全年指引上调，才说明 Cortex Code 和 Snowflake Intelligence 正在比基线更快地拉动 consumption。来源：TIKR。

这次财报的新信息不是“Snowflake 有 AI story”，而是“AI story 已经开始落到产品收入和全年指引里”。在 Snowflake 的 consumption model 里，产品收入比 logo 数、试用账户数更硬，因为它来自客户对 compute、storage、data transfer 的实际消耗。Q1 产品收入 13.34 亿美元、同比 34%，明显高于此前约 27% 的 Q1 产品收入指引；全年产品收入指引从 56.60 亿美元上调到 58.40 亿美元；同时非 GAAP operating margin 指引从 12.5% 上调到 13.5%。这组合回答了两个财报前疑问：AI 不只是 adoption metric，而且至少这一季没有以牺牲经营杠杆为代价。

财报前市场有三个压制因素。第一，股价此前仍承压。TS2/TechStock² 写道，SNOW 年内下跌 21.5%，且估值为 forward sales 约 9.4 倍，高于互联网软件行业 3.81 倍；因此“less room for error”。来源：TS2 财报前分析。第二，AI 投入的毛利和现金流担忧真实存在。TIKR 引述管理层此前承认 AI 产品当前毛利低于核心 analytics workloads，只有当 AI consumption scale 快于拖累时，这个 trade-off 才是正面的。来源：TIKR。第三，期权市场虽预期大波动，但方向不确定；TipRanks 称期权隐含约 13.59% 单日波动，高于近四季平均 11.85%。来源：TipRanks 摘要 via Exa。

因此，盘后 +30% 级别的上涨可以理解为三层预期差同时释放：

  1. 数字预期差：EPS 0.39 vs 0.32，收入 13.9 亿 vs 13.2 亿。
  2. 指引预期差：Q2 产品收入高于共识，全年产品收入从 56.6 亿提到 58.4 亿。
  3. 叙事预期差：AI 从“已知受益方向但货币化仍需证明”变成“推动核心平台消费、带来历史最强 sequential product revenue dollar growth 的动力”。

需要保留的矛盾与风险

RPO 的解读不完全单向。官方口径是 RPO 92.1 亿美元、同比增长 38%，但 IBD 同时指出市场估计为 92.6 亿美元，略低于预期。来源：IBD。这意味着上涨并不是因为所有指标都完美，而是产品收入、AI adoption、指引上修的权重更高。

GAAP 仍亏损。StockTitan 8-K 摘要显示 GAAP net loss 为 2.956 亿美元，每股亏损 0.86 美元；公司仍不是 GAAP 盈利企业。来源：StockTitan 8-K 页面。盘后上涨反映的是增长与未来现金流预期重估，不是 GAAP 盈利拐点已经完成。

盘后价格需要动态更新。本文采集时间接近财报发布后一小时内，不同来源显示 +23% 至 +34% 不等。若用于交易或记录最终收盘，需要次日常规交易价格确认。

结论

SNOW 今天盘后大涨，是因为这份财报同时回答了市场最关心的三个问题：核心 consumption 是否仍强、AI 是否转化为实际消费、增长再加速是否足以抵消毛利/估值担忧。答案短期看是 yes。真正的 surprise 在于 Snowflake 不只是 beat 了 Q1，而是把 FY2027 产品收入增长预期从 27% 提到 31%，并同时上调经营利润率指引；这使市场不得不重新定价“Snowflake 是一个增长降速的软件公司”这个前提。

Snowflake (SNOW) Q1 FY2027 Earnings and After-Hours Rally

Date: 2026-05-27
Topic: Snowflake's latest earnings, after-hours stock move, why the stock rallied, and what surprised the market

Core Takeaway

Snowflake's after-hours rally was not driven by a simple EPS beat. It was the combination of three things: revenue and product revenue beat expectations, full-year product revenue guidance was raised from 27% growth to 31% growth, and AI product usage began to look like a real consumption-revenue driver rather than just a narrative. Before the print, investors worried that Snowflake's growth was decelerating and that AI investments would pressure margins and cash flow. This report delivered the opposite signal: reaccelerating growth plus a higher non-GAAP operating margin outlook.

After-hours quote sources differed by timestamp. MarketBeat showed SNOW at $233.59 at 4:51pm ET, up $58.99 or 33.79% from the $174.60 regular-session close. Google Finance showed $235.20 at 4:52pm ET, up $59.94 or 34.20%. CNBC showed $215.92 at 4:06pm ET, up $40.66 or 23.20%. IBD reported the stock up more than 30% to around $231.20. Because after-hours liquidity is thin and prices moved quickly, the most robust description is that SNOW rose roughly 23% to 34%, later stabilizing around a 30%+ move. Sources: MarketBeat, Google Finance, CNBC, IBD.

What Beat Expectations

Snowflake released Q1 FY2027 results through its IR site, Business Wire, and 8-K materials. The quarter ended April 30, 2026. The official IR release and StockTitan transcript show product revenue of $1.334 billion, up 34% year over year; total revenue of $1.391 billion, up 33%; net revenue retention of 126%; RPO of $9.21 billion, up 38%; and 779 customers with trailing 12-month product revenue above $1 million, up 29%. Sources: Snowflake IR, StockTitan press release mirror, StockTitan 8-K page.

Management's language was unusually strong. CEO Sridhar Ramaswamy said:

"Snowflake delivered a milestone quarter, with product revenue of $1.33 billion, up 34% year-over-year, marking the strongest sequential dollar growth in our history... AI continues to be a powerful tailwind for Snowflake, and Q1 marks a clear inflection point in that journey."

CFO Brian Robins tied the full-year guide raise directly to both the core platform and AI momentum:

"Given the strong momentum across both our core platform business and AI business, we are raising our full-year product revenue guidance."

Relative to consensus, IBD reported adjusted EPS of $0.39 versus Wall Street expectations of $0.32, and revenue of $1.39 billion versus $1.32 billion expected. MarketBeat showed the same basic setup: actual EPS of $0.39 versus consensus of $0.32, a $0.07 beat; actual revenue of $1.39 billion versus expected revenue of $1.32 billion, a beat of about $67.14 million. Sources: IBD, MarketBeat.

Guidance Was the Real Catalyst

The stock reaction was mainly about forward guidance. Snowflake guided Q2 FY2027 product revenue to $1.415 billion to $1.420 billion, above IBD's cited consensus of $1.374 billion. For the full year, Snowflake raised FY2027 product revenue guidance to $5.840 billion, or 31% growth, up from prior guidance of $5.660 billion, or 27% growth. Sources: IBD, official release mirror.

That matters because, before the print, investors were not merely asking whether Snowflake could beat one quarter. They were asking whether AI-driven usage could push consumption above the baseline and make the prior 27% full-year growth guide look conservative. TIKR put it clearly before earnings:

"The market will not react to a number that simply matches guidance. The real tells are RPO trajectory and full-year guidance... A full-year guidance raise would signal that Cortex Code and Snowflake Intelligence are pulling consumption above the baseline ahead of schedule."

Source: TIKR pre-earnings analysis. The actual result hit that bull case: Q1 beat, and full-year product revenue guidance moved from 27% to 31% growth.

Why the Stock Rose

First, product revenue beat decisively. Snowflake is a consumption-based business, so product revenue is more directly tied to customer usage than total revenue. The official filing defines product revenue as revenue primarily from customer consumption of compute, storage, and data transfer resources, rather than traditional subscription revenue recognized ratably over a contract term. Q1 product revenue of $1.334 billion, up 34%, was far above the prior Q1 product revenue guide of $1.262 billion to $1.267 billion. That earlier guide came from the Q4 FY2026 official 8-K, which guided Q1 FY2027 product revenue to 27% growth and full-year product revenue to $5.660 billion, also 27% growth. Source: SEC Q4 FY2026 8-K / earnings release.

Second, the full-year guide raise changed the growth narrative. Before earnings, the bear case was that Snowflake was fading from roughly 29%-30% growth into the mid-20s, while AI remained more narrative than revenue. AInvest framed the bull case as a Q1 revenue beat plus a full-year guide raise, with FY2027 growth staying above 28%; the bear case was revenue at or below the $1.32 billion consensus, a guidance cut, or growth settling into the low 20s. Source: AInvest pre-earnings analysis. The actual 31% full-year product revenue growth guide landed clearly in the bull-case zone.

Third, AI products began to look like evidence of consumption growth rather than just product announcements. The official materials said more than 13,600 accounts were using Snowflake AI capabilities, accounts using Snowflake Intelligence more than doubled quarter over quarter, and Cortex Code was already used by more than 7,100 accounts. The same source said these innovations contributed to the strongest sequential product revenue dollar growth in company history. Source: StockTitan 8-K page. This directly addressed the pre-earnings question: can AI features translate into heavier platform consumption?

Fourth, AI investment did not appear to crush profitability. The official release showed Q1 non-GAAP operating income of $165.8 million, an 11.9% margin, and adjusted free cash flow of $265.5 million, a 19.1% margin. Full-year non-GAAP operating margin guidance was raised to 13.5% from 12.5%. Source: official release mirror. That eased concerns that AI workloads and the Observe acquisition would weigh on margins and free cash flow.

Why It Was Surprising

The surprise was not that nobody expected a beat. There were bullish checks before the print, and some analysts already expected Snowflake to come in above guidance. The surprise was the scale and quality of the beat: product revenue moved from prior guidance of $1.262-$1.267 billion to an actual $1.334 billion; full-year product revenue guidance moved from 27% growth to 31%; and operating margin guidance also went up.

More precisely, the market did not suddenly decide today that AI could be a growth signal for Snowflake. AI benefit was already part of the consensus, and it was already embedded in bullish pre-earnings commentary. CRN wrote after Q4 that Snowflake was entering the new fiscal year on more stable growth footing, with notable successes in its AI product portfolio. The same article noted Wall Street expected about $5.55 billion of FY2027 product revenue, while Snowflake had guided to about $5.66 billion; Bank of America believed Snowflake could deliver at least 30% growth. Source: CRN. Reuters' February headline also framed the annual product revenue outlook as stronger because AI was boosting demand. Source: The Globe and Mail / Reuters.

What was not fully consensus was whether AI adoption could become durable, predictable consumption revenue without sacrificing margins. That is why the pre-earnings debate was not "does Snowflake have AI?" but "does AI actually monetize?" Barchart's pre-earnings headline was direct: "Snowflake: The Market Wants Proof That AI Products Actually Monetize." It said investors would scrutinize whether Snowflake's agentic AI capabilities and enterprise governance platform could translate into durable long-term growth. Source: Barchart. TIKR made the same point in a narrower way: a full-year guide raise would signal that Cortex Code and Snowflake Intelligence were pulling consumption above the baseline ahead of schedule. Source: TIKR.

So the new information was not "Snowflake has an AI story." It was that the AI story had begun to land in product revenue and full-year guidance. In Snowflake's consumption model, product revenue is harder evidence than logos or trial accounts because it comes from actual consumption of compute, storage, and data transfer. The combination of 34% product revenue growth, full-year product revenue guidance rising from $5.660 billion to $5.840 billion, and non-GAAP operating margin guidance rising from 12.5% to 13.5% answered two questions at once: AI was not just an adoption metric, and at least this quarter it did not require sacrificing operating leverage.

Before earnings, three factors had been weighing on sentiment. First, the stock had been under pressure. TS2/TechStock2 wrote that SNOW was down 21.5% year to date and traded at 9.4 times forward 12-month sales, above the internet software industry average of 3.81 times, leaving "less room for error." Source: TS2 pre-earnings analysis. Second, AI margin and cash-flow concerns were real. TIKR noted that management had acknowledged AI products currently carry lower gross margins than core analytics workloads; the trade-off only works if AI consumption scales faster than the margin drag. Source: TIKR. Third, options priced a large move but not direction. TipRanks said options implied about a 13.59% move, above Snowflake's average post-earnings move of 11.85% over the prior four quarters. Source: TipRanks.

The roughly 30% after-hours rally can therefore be understood as three expectation gaps closing at once:

  1. Numbers: EPS of $0.39 versus $0.32 expected; revenue of $1.39 billion versus $1.32 billion expected.
  2. Guidance: Q2 product revenue above consensus, and full-year product revenue raised from $5.66 billion to $5.84 billion.
  3. Narrative: AI moved from "known tailwind, monetization still needs proof" to "evidence of stronger core platform consumption and the strongest sequential product revenue dollar growth in company history."

Caveats and Risks

RPO was not an entirely clean positive. Snowflake reported RPO of $9.21 billion, up 38%, but IBD noted that estimates were around $9.26 billion, meaning RPO was slightly below that estimate. Source: IBD. This means the stock did not rally because every metric was perfect. Product revenue, AI adoption, and the full-year guide raise carried more weight.

Snowflake is still GAAP unprofitable. StockTitan's 8-K summary showed GAAP net loss of $295.6 million, or $0.86 per share. Source: StockTitan 8-K page. The after-hours move reflects a re-rating of growth and future cash-flow expectations, not a completed GAAP profitability inflection.

After-hours prices should also be treated as dynamic. This report captured data within roughly the first hour after the earnings release, and sources showed moves ranging from about +23% to +34%. For trading or final recordkeeping, the next regular-session close matters more.

Conclusion

SNOW rallied after hours because this report answered the three questions investors cared about most: is core consumption still strong, is AI converting into real usage, and is growth reaccelerating enough to offset margin and valuation concerns? The near-term answer was yes. The real surprise was not merely that Snowflake beat Q1 numbers. It was that FY2027 product revenue growth expectations moved from 27% to 31% while operating margin guidance also rose, forcing the market to reprice the assumption that Snowflake was simply a decelerating software company.
